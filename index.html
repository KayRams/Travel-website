The City of Gold

Welcome to Johannesburg

Commonly known as Jozi, eGoli, Maboneng. Johannesburg is the largest city in South Africa. It was founded in 1886 following the discovery of gold on the Witwatersrand. This event triggered a massive gold rush, attracting thousands of prospectors, labourers and fortune seekers from around the world. The rapid influx of people and the establishment of mining operations led to the city's fast development. Johannesburg is the economic hub of South Africa and the continent. It is home to the Johannesburg Stock Exchange(JSE) and the richest square mile in Africa - Sandton. Sandton is a financial district, its status as the "richest square mile" comes from the concetration of wealth, corporate headquaters, and financial activity within a relatively small area, making it a symbol of economic power in Africa.

THE BEST THINGS TO DO
NELSON MANDELA SQUARE

This award-winning shopping centre, adjoined to Sandton City is located in the heart of South Africa’s financial district. It features a large public square with a famous 6-meter bronze statue of Nelson Mandela, symbolizing his legacy and South Africa's democracy. The square has a diverse array of fine dining restaurants and sidewalk cafés, offering a sophisticated and cosmopolitan experience. Sandton City is a shopping centre tailored for a unique shopping experience, with over 300 of the world’s most in-demand brands. From an eco-fashionable Food District, a family-focussed Fun District, to a chandelier draped Diamond Walk which is home to many global luxury brands. Each year Sandton City is frequented by millions of locals and tourists alike, those with a flare to experience the best of South African retail.

HAVE AN AUTHENTIC JO'BURG EXPERIENCE
MABONENG PRECINCT

Once labelled one of the coolest neighbourhoods in the world (according to Forbes magazine), Maboneng was a golden child among Joburg’s regeneration stories after it was transformed from an industrial and factory district to a buzzing lifestyle playground. The development of the Maboneng Precinct began in 2008 and now, this colourful and youthful neighbourhood has cemented its place as a popular Joburg visitors attraction. Maboneng is known for a mix of culture, entertainment and a unique selection of bars, cafés and restaurants. One of its main attractions is Arts on Main, a creative hub where artists showcase their work. Maboneng has now become a major nightlife hotspot and Saturdays the streets are thronged with a young party crowd.
